Governance infrastructure for agentic systems

The layer that decides whether an AI action deserves release.

cgmm3-govern sits beside an agent, API, or workflow runner and checks proposed actions before they are shown, executed, or logged as trusted outputs. It turns governance into a product surface: explicit policy gates, machine-readable receipts, audit persistence, and a formal contest path.

Why it exists

The product is the governance layer, not the benchmark.

What most agent stacks already do

Planning, prompts, tool orchestration, UX, and action generation. They can decide what they want to do, but they still need a clean control boundary before those actions become trusted outputs.

What cgmm3-govern adds

A reusable control plane for explicit policy checks, fairness and uncertainty gates, receipts, audit trails, and reviewer workflows. The host stays flexible. The governance contract stays stable.

Why the repo matters

The surrounding research pipeline proves this is not abstract policy theater. It shows the governance layer sitting on top of real treatment-effect outputs, overlap checks, subgroup gaps, and deployment gates.
